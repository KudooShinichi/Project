IONS

For ions, the charge on a particular atom may be denoted with a superscript. For example Na+ or Cu2+.The total charge on a charged molecule or a polyatomic icon may be shown in this way. For example:H3O+ or S042-.

For more complex ions, brackets[ ] are often used to enclose the ionic formula, as in [B12H12]2-, which is found in compounds such as Cs2[B12H12].Parentheses () can be nested inside brackets to indicate a repeating unit, as in [Co(NH3)6]3+. Here (NH3)6, indicates that the ion contains six NH3 groups, and [] encloses the entire formula of the ion with change +3.
